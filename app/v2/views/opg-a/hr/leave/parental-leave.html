Parental leave policy

  • Content owner: MoJ HR
  • Last updated: 27 March 2016

What you need to know

Parental leave is unpaid time off to look after your child or make arrangements for their welfare.

Eligible parents can take unpaid parental leave up until the child’s 18th birthday.

You can take parental leave if:

  • you’ve been employed by the MoJ for at least a year

  • you’re the parent named on the child’s birth or adoption certificate

  • you have legal responsibility for a child under 18

Parental leave can be taken in blocks of one week only (unless you have responsibility for a disabled child).
You must normally give at least 21 days’ notice.

Your manager must authorise your request and will agree your leave dates with you.

What you need to do

Send your manager the request parental leave letter doc, and include:

  • dates of the proposed leave
  • age of the child (you’ll need to provide evidence)
  • evidence of your legal responsibility for the child
  • total parental leave you’ve previously taken
