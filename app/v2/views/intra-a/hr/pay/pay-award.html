Pay Award 2015

The pay award came into effect on 1 August.

FAQs

  • Section 1 – general information pdf
  • Section 2 – bands A-D pdf
  • Section 3 – bands E & F pdf
  • Section 4 – staff on legacy terms pdf
  • Section 5 – fast stream staff pdf

Bands A – D

For this staff group the pay award focuses on top performers. So the pay award will be based on annual performance markings (2014/15 – performance year).

  • 2014/15 – performance year table pdf

In addition a non-consolidated performance related award will be given to staff with an outstanding mark payable as the 2014 award.

  • ‘Outstanding’ mark table pdf

Pay ranges are being maintained at current values (with the exception of the pay range min at Band D). There will be no changes to pay range maxima.

Band D (All pay ranges)

We are raising the pay range minima (min) for key first line manager posts at Band D by £500. This continues the action to protect our lower paid staff undertaken in the 2013 and 2014 pay award and will address the position where a number of first line managers find themselves earning less than the staff they manage, due to the way salaries have operated during the pay freeze.

  • Band minima table pdf

Bands E – F

Staff on the Band E & F closed pay range (i.e. pre 1/8/13) who have yet to reach their respective pay range maxima (max) will continue to see step-related pay increases until they reach their max. Staff on the 2013 open pay ranges (i.e. post 1/8/13) will receive a performance-related consolidated award, payable at the same rates as in Bands A-D. The 2013 open pay ranges will have their pay range min uplifted, any staff positioned below the new pay band minima will be repositioned to the new min following the pay award.

  • Band E and F minima table pdf

No changes to pay range maxima will occur. This award seeks to protect our lowest paid staff by raising the starting salaries across the open Band E and F pay ranges.
